IoT Based Railway Fishplate and Track Monitoring System for Safe Transport [On Going]

This project is my main focus of my working as a research assistant at BUET. In 2019, I along with Professor Dr. Md Farhad Hossain, Department of EEE, BUET gave a research proposal to University Grants Commission (UGC) to perform a research and develop a state-of-the-art system to solve a major issue in the railway track system of Bangladesh using Internet of Things (IoT) technology. We have received a grant of 3,00,000 Taka (eq. 3534 Dollars) from UGC for the year of 2020.

  • The primary objective of this project is to design an online system to detect and locate any unstable/removed fishplate and uprooted rail tracks.

  • This IoT based system can monitor every fishplate of the track in real-time server and is highly accurate to detect and localize any displaced fishplate.

  • In case of any unwanted event occurs, a signal/message will be sent to the central monitoring system and associated fishplates and tracks will be displayed in a digital map.

Project affiliation/Sponsor(s): Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET), University Grants Commission (UGC)

IoT Based Wristband and Contact Tracing App for the Protection from COVID-19 [On Going]

After the COVID-19 outbreak happened, our "Team Hawkbit" along with Professor Dr. Md Farhad Hossain, Department of EEE, BUET had decided to build a QR code based app to restrict the movement of corona affected patient. We are currently working on this project.

  • A restriction strategy using a contact tracing mobile app data that is interactive via Bluetooth when installed between two phones.

  • Location data and contact IDs are stored at a secured database in the cloud service to find the travel history of the patient at a later period if needed.

  • QR codes which are red, green and yellow are generated for the people to restrict the patient/possible patient away from the healthy people.

Project affiliation/Sponsor(s): Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET), Beximco Inc., Walton group

A Cyber-secured MQTT Based Offline Automation System

It was a collaborative project between ACI Logistics Limited and Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET). The objective of this project was to build a desktop software which can monitor and control a supershop's electrical compliances using Internet of Things (IoT). Our "Team Hawkbit" along with Professor Dr. Md Farhad Hossain, Department of EEE, BUET worked on this project for six months and successfully developed the project and run the demo version of it. Our project related research paper titled ‘‘A Cyber-Secured MQTT based Offline Automation System,’’ had been published in IEEE 2019 International Conference on Wireless Communications Signal Processing and Networking (WiSPNET). , Chennai, India. I participated in the conference and presented our research paper at that conference. [pdf]

  • Developed an MQTT based offline system that can superstore, warehouses buildings and factories etc. which are integrated into a single dashboard where monitoring and controlling can be simultaneously executed.

  • Utilized locally normalized Histogram Oriented Gradient descriptors to detect human from an image.

  • Built a python based IoT software which can monitor and control all the electrical appliances of a super shop.

Project affiliation/Sponsor(s): Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET), ACI Logistics Limited

Rice Panicle Segmentation

We have developed a Gaussian mixture model based unsupervised image segmentation algorithm that can segment rice panicles. The proposed method outperforms existing methods like Panicle-SEG with an average F1 score greater than 80%.

Practical Wireless Communication Problems

After finishing my undergraduate studies I started working as a System Engineer at Grameenphone, part of the Norwegian Telenor Group, is the largest telecommunications operator in Bangladesh. I solved some interesting problems related to radio access and microwave backhaul there. A simple example is illustrated here.

 

A particular cell of a BTS was suffering for low MPD and as a reason we found that the cell was overshooting at long distances. Which can be understood from measurement reports (MR) of TA = 21 to 31. The cell was using Kathrein 739636 antenna. This antenna has 6 degree fixed electrical downtilt. The planned mechanical uptilt was -3 degree. After changing the mechanical tilting from -3 degree to 0 degree the number MRs of TA between 21 to 31 reduced significantly yielding regular MPD. The red line indicates the moment we changed the mechanical tilting.

Stochastic Modeling of Wireless Network

We used Poisson point process (PPP) and Hard-core poisson process (HCPP) to simulate BTS locations and evaluated various network parameters like throughput, SINR, outage probability using Monte Carlo simulation incorporating the idea of resource blocks. We used WINNER II channel model for path loss.
